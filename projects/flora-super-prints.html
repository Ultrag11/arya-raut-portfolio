Operations Internship | Flora Super Prints

Improving bottle flow, safety, and scheduling in a screen-printing facility.

Flora Super Prints was a screen-printing manufacturing facility specializing in cosmetic glass bottles. My work focused on material movement, inventory flow, worker safety, and production scheduling across labeling, curing furnace, and packaging operations.

Problem

Cosmetic glass bottles moved through multiple process stages: labeling, curing furnace, and packaging. The movement between these areas created manual handling effort, stack confusion, ergonomic strain, and scheduling challenges whenever worker availability changed.

What I Implemented

I designed an ergonomic trolley to move bottles between process stages, reducing the need for repeated manual lifting and carrying. I also reorganized bottle stacks by order deadlines and applied Kanban principles so workers could see what needed to move next without searching through inventory.

For labor utilization, I used Excel OpenSolver to optimize labeling team schedules. The model helped reduce the impact of worker absences and improved how operators were assigned to available equipment.

Impact

  • Reduced manual bottle handling time by 18.93% through ergonomic trolley design.
  • Improved worker safety by 35.38% based on NASA-TLX assessment.
  • Saved 140 minutes per worker daily by reorganizing bottle stacks around order deadlines.
  • Improved operator equipment efficiency by 11% through Excel OpenSolver schedule optimization.

Internship Work

Three practical improvements across the production floor.

The internship was not one isolated improvement. It connected physical handling, visual inventory control, and labor scheduling into one flow problem: how to move bottles safely, clearly, and on time.

Ergonomic trolley design

I designed a trolley for transporting cosmetic glass bottles between labeling, curing, and packaging. The goal was to reduce repetitive manual handling while keeping bottles organized during movement.

Kanban inventory flow

I reorganized bottle stacks by order deadlines and used Kanban thinking to make priority work easier to identify, reducing search time and supporting on-time delivery.

OpenSolver scheduling

I built an Excel OpenSolver schedule model for the labeling team so the operation could respond better to worker absences and improve how equipment time was used.
